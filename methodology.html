How We Score Supplements

Every product in Formulate gets a score from 0 to 100. No opinions, no sponsorships, no guesswork — just clinical evidence, dose science, and transparent methodology. Here's exactly how it works.

Evidence Quality (25%)

This is the most heavily weighted pillar because no supplement matters if the science doesn't support it. We grade each ingredient's evidence on a four-tier scale:

GradeRequirementsExamples
A 3+ meta-analyses or systematic reviews with consistent results Vitamin D, Creatine, Omega-3
B 1+ well-designed RCTs with supportive observational data Ashwagandha, Magnesium, Lion's Mane
C Preliminary RCTs or observational studies with mixed results Emerging nootropics, newer botanicals
D Animal/in-vitro only or insufficient human data Novel compounds, traditional remedies

A product's evidence score is the weighted average of all its ingredients' evidence grades. Products with mostly Grade A ingredients score highest.

Dose Adequacy (20%)

A supplement can have the best ingredients in the world, but if they're underdosed, you're wasting money. We compare each ingredient's dose against evidence-backed reference ranges:

  • Therapeutic (100) — Within the clinically proven effective range
  • Optimal (90) — Within the optimal longevity range
  • RDA Met (80) — Meets the baseline daily requirement
  • Below RDA (70) — Below the official minimum recommendation
  • Severely Below (60) — Significantly underdosed
  • Over Limit (70) — Exceeds established upper safety limits
  • Undisclosed (50) — Hidden behind a proprietary blend

Proprietary blend penalty: If more than 50% of a product's ingredient doses are undisclosed (hidden in a proprietary blend), the entire product score is capped at 50 regardless of other factors.

Bioavailability & Forms (15%)

Not all forms of an ingredient are equal. Magnesium oxide absorbs at roughly 4%, while magnesium glycinate absorbs at over 80%. We score each ingredient form based on absorption research:

Score RangeQualityExample (Magnesium)
95-100 Best form — excellent absorption, bioactive Glycinate, L-Threonate
85-94 Very good — reliable absorption Taurate, Malate, Citrate
70-84 Adequate — moderate absorption Carbonate
Below 70 Poor — limited absorption, avoid Oxide (40)

We maintain a comprehensive database of ingredient form scores covering hundreds of variants across vitamins, minerals, amino acids, and botanical extracts.

Manufacturing Quality (20%)

Manufacturing quality reflects whether a company actually delivers what's on the label:

  • GMP Certified (+40) — Manufactured in a Good Manufacturing Practices facility
  • Third-Party Tested (+30) — Independently verified by NSF, USP, or ConsumerLab
  • Independently Verified (+20) — Additional quality certifications
  • Clean Label (+10) — Free from unnecessary artificial additives

Transparency (10%)

We reward brands that tell you exactly what's in the bottle:

  • Individual amounts disclosed (+40) — Every ingredient dose listed separately
  • No proprietary blends (+30) — Nothing hidden behind a total blend weight
  • Specific forms stated (+20) — e.g., "Magnesium Glycinate" not just "Magnesium"
  • Third-party verification (+10) — Independent testing confirms label accuracy

Proprietary blends — where a company lists ingredients but hides individual amounts — receive a -30 point penalty.

Safety (10%)

Safety scoring accounts for known risks:

  • Drug interactions — Products containing high-interaction ingredients (St. John's Wort, Ginkgo biloba, high-dose Vitamin K) are penalized
  • Upper limit violations — Doses exceeding established safety limits are flagged
  • Missing warnings — Products that should carry interaction or contraindication warnings but don't

Time-Weighted Accountability

Recalls and regulatory actions are time-weighted — recent events have more impact than older ones. A recall from last year matters more than one from seven years ago, reflecting a brand's current practices rather than ancient history.

Confidence Indicators

Every brand score includes a confidence indicator based on data completeness. Brands with limited data are marked as provisional so you know when a score is based on partial information.

Our Principles

Deterministic

The same product always gets the same score. No manual overrides, no subjective adjustments. The algorithm is the authority.

Evidence-Based

Scores are grounded in published research — meta-analyses, RCTs, and systematic reviews from PubMed, Examine.com, and other peer-reviewed sources. Marketing claims are ignored.

Transparent

Every score component is visible. You can see exactly how much each pillar contributed to the final number. No black boxes.

Independent

Formulate does not accept payments from supplement manufacturers. Brand partnerships, sponsorships, and advertising have zero influence on scores. We may earn affiliate commissions when you purchase products through our links, but this never affects how products are scored. See our affiliate disclosure for details.

Versioned

Our scoring algorithm is versioned and tracked. When we improve the methodology, all products are rescored to maintain consistency.

Data sources: PubMed/MEDLINE, USDA FoodData Central, Examine.com, ConsumerLab, FDA databases, NSF International, USP
