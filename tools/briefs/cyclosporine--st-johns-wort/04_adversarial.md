# Stage 4 — Adversarial Review

Pair: **Cyclosporine** + **St John's wort**

## 1. Source substitution attack

| Claim | Tag | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| 2 heart transplant rejections after SJW start (Ruschitzka 2000) | [moderate, S1] | SUPPORTED |
| 4-patient case series, cyclosporine 25-62% drop, rejection in all | [moderate, S2] | SUPPORTED |
| Borrelli 2007 review catalogs multiple cases | [moderate, S3] | SUPPORTED |
| Hyperforin → PXR → CYP3A4 + P-gp induction | [strong, S4] | SUPPORTED |

All tags stand.

## 2. Directness attack

All 4 sources directly address SJW + cyclosporine/calcineurin inhibitors in humans or hepatocyte models. HIGH directness across the board.

## 3. Contradiction search

No RCT has deliberately co-administered (would be unethical given documented harm). No case report exists arguing SJW is safe with cyclosporine. No major transplant center recommends anything other than contraindication. Uncontested.

## 4. Dose + population attack

- Draft extends contraindication to tacrolimus and sirolimus based on shared CYP3A4 metabolism. Class mechanism argument. Defensible — tacrolimus has independent case reports of SJW-induced rejection.
- Dose: standard retail SJW (600-900 mg/day) has caused the interaction. Low-hyperforin products noted but explicitly excluded from safe-use recommendation given non-reversible consequence.

## 5. Severity mis-tagging attack

Original: DANGER. Draft: DANGER (hold). Audit:
- Non-reversible harm? Graft rejection and possible graft loss. YES.
- Emergency presentation? Acute rejection is a transplant-medicine emergency. YES.
- Effective mitigation short of avoidance? None reliable.

DANGER holds. No downgrade defensible.

## 6. Timing advice plausibility

CYP3A4 induction is transcriptional; takes days to develop and 1-2 weeks to resolve. Hour-by-hour timing is irrelevant. SUPPORTED.

## 7. Publication-bias probe

- S1 Ruschitzka 2000: Lancet — top-tier.
- S2 Mai-Bonde 2001: Am J Kidney Dis.
- S3 Borrelli 2007: widely cited review.
- S4 Nicolussi 2020: Br J Pharmacol.

Diverse, high-quality.

## 8. Retraction check

- S1 PMID 10683008: not retracted.
- S2 PMID 11684566: not retracted.
- S3 PMID 17486092: not retracted.
- S4 PMID 31943241: not retracted.

## Summary

All claims survive. DANGER holds. This is one of the clearest cases in the list — multiple independent case series, documented non-reversible harm, well-characterized mechanism, unanimous expert consensus.
