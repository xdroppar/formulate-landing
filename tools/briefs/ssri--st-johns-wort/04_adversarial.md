# Stage 4 — Adversarial Review

Pair: **SSRI** + **St John's wort**

## 1. Source substitution attack

| Claim | Tag | Source check | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| SJW + SSRI causes symptoms of central serotonin excess | [moderate, S1] | Borrelli 2007 states verbatim. | SUPPORTED |
| Pharmacodynamic serotonergic synergy, not purely PK | [moderate, S1] / [moderate, S2] | Both explicitly argue shared pathway. | SUPPORTED |
| Low-hyperforin does NOT eliminate risk | [moderate, S2] | Nicolussi 2020 notes PK interactions reduced but serotonergic effect unchanged. | SUPPORTED |
| Sertraline/paroxetine most commonly reported, doses 20-75 mg; SJW 600-900 mg | [moderate, S3] | StatPearls + search corroborate. | SUPPORTED |
| "Do not combine" recommendation | [moderate, S1] / [moderate, S3] | Direct in S1 and S3. | SUPPORTED |

No UNSUPPORTED claims.

## 2. Directness attack

- All 3 sources directly address the SJW-SSRI pair in humans.
- No population extrapolation from animal/in-vitro.
- Primary evidence is spontaneous adverse-event reporting, which has lower directness than RCT but HIGH directness for the specific pair.
- No downgrade.

## 3. Contradiction search

- **Negative-finding search attempted**: `"St John's wort" AND SSRI AND ("no effect" OR "safe" OR "no interaction")` — returns no RCT showing safety of co-administration. There is no contradicting evidence arguing the combination is safe.
- **Is there a "mild cases only" minority view?** Some reviews note that many case reports are mild. But even the mild-case reviewers do NOT argue combination is safe — they argue severity is heterogeneous. This is not contradicting evidence.
- Nicolussi 2020 is the strongest "tempering" voice but only for PK interactions. On serotonin-syndrome grounds the review still recommends against combining.

No contradiction found that forces downgrade.

## 4. Dose + population attack

- **Population over-extension**: Draft claims "any patient on any SSRI." Evidence base includes sertraline, paroxetine, fluoxetine, citalopram — the most-prescribed SSRIs. Mechanism (shared serotonergic activity) applies to all SSRIs by pharmacology. Extrapolation to escitalopram, fluvoxamine is by class mechanism. Defensible.
- **Dose under-specification**: Draft says "no safe dose established." That's correct — even the lowest doses in case reports (SJW 600 mg, SSRI 20 mg) have produced syndromes. Specifying "no safe dose" is more honest than naming a threshold.

## 5. Severity mis-tagging attack

Original: DANGER. Draft: DANGER. Audit:
- Non-reversible harm documented? Serotonin syndrome can progress to death from hyperthermia/rhabdo/DIC. YES.
- Emergency presentation? Hyperthermia >41°C and seizures qualify. YES.
- Effective mitigation short of discontinuation? No.

DANGER holds. No downgrade warranted.

## 6. Timing-advice plausibility

Draft says timing does not mitigate. SSRIs reach steady-state in 1-5 weeks and have half-lives of 20+ hours (fluoxetine and its active metabolite especially). SJW hypericin/hyperforin half-lives are 9-43 hours. Spacing by hours is meaningless when both agents are at steady-state concentrations. Mechanistically correct. SUPPORTED.

## 7. Publication-bias probe

- S1 Borrelli & Izzo: Italian academic group. Independent.
- S2 Nicolussi et al: Swiss academic group. Independent.
- S3 StatPearls: multiple contributors, peer-reviewed, frequently updated.
- Not concentrated, not industry-funded.

## 8. Retraction check

- S1 PMID 17486092: not retracted (2026-04-21).
- S2 PMID 31943241: not retracted.
- S3 StatPearls: living chapter.

## Stage 4 summary

All 3 claims survive. Severity holds at DANGER. No softening needed. Ready for Stage 5.
