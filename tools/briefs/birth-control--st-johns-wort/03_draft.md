# Stage 3 — Draft

Pair: **Birth control** + **St John's wort**

## Summary

St John's wort reduces oral contraceptive effectiveness and has caused breakthrough bleeding and unintended pregnancies.

## Details

### Mechanism

Hyperforin, a primary constituent of St John's wort (*Hypericum perforatum*), activates the pregnane X receptor (PXR) and induces cytochrome P450 3A4 and P-glycoprotein [strong, S3]. CYP3A4 is the enzyme that metabolizes ethinylestradiol and most progestins in combined oral contraceptives; inducing it accelerates contraceptive clearance and lowers circulating hormone levels [strong, S3]. P-glycoprotein induction additionally reduces intestinal absorption of affected drugs [strong, S3]. Net effect: less hormone in circulation, less ovulation suppression, more endometrial breakthrough [moderate, S1].

### Evidence quality

Unlike most supplement-drug interactions, this one has a **direct RCT**. Pfrunder 2003 [moderate, S1] gave 17 healthy women a low-dose combined OC (0.02 mg ethinylestradiol + 0.150 mg desogestrel) across three crossover cycles: OC alone, OC + SJW 300 mg twice daily, OC + SJW 300 mg three times daily. Breakthrough bleeding rose from 6/17 (35%) on OC alone to 13/17 (77%, p<0.015) at the BID SJW dose and to 15/17 (88%, p<0.001) at TID. The dose-response pattern is explicit. Ovulation was not detected (progesterone stayed suppressed), but follicle-like structures appeared sporadically — meaning the contraceptive margin narrows.

Pharmacovigilance data confirm the clinical impact: by December 2001 the UK regulator had received 7 reports of unintended pregnancies attributed to SJW + OC co-use, with 2 additional cases from Sweden [moderate, S2]. All nine women had used OCs without problems for at least seven months before adding SJW, strengthening temporal attribution [moderate, S2]. A 2024 systematic review (S4) confirms the same picture with additional accumulated cases.

### Clinical relevance

The breakthrough-bleeding rate more than doubled at a standard SJW dose (600 mg/day) and nearly tripled at a high dose (900 mg/day) [moderate, S1]. Unintended pregnancy is the non-reversible outcome that defines this as a DANGER-tier interaction — no timing workaround, no dose separation, no rescue once the contraceptive has failed [moderate, S2] [moderate, S4].

### Dose considerations

Hyperforin content determines PK-interaction severity. Products with >1 mg hyperforin/day are considered high-risk; a low-hyperforin extract (Will-Shahab 2009, cited in S3) showed no measurable PK interaction [moderate, S3]. However, most retail SJW products are high-hyperforin, and hyperforin content is rarely disclosed on US supplement labels. Default assumption: any SJW product carries interaction risk unless the specific product and dose have been confirmed low-hyperforin.

### Populations to watch

- **Women on combined oral contraceptives**: highest evidence base.
- **Women on progestin-only pills, patches, rings, implants, IUDs**: CYP3A4 induction affects hormonal contraceptives broadly; progestin-only methods rely on narrower hormone margins and may be MORE susceptible to induction-driven failure, though direct trial data are sparse.
- **Women on combination patches or rings**: same mechanism as combined OCs; same caution.
- **Emergency contraception**: levonorgestrel emergency contraception is CYP3A4-metabolized; SJW can reduce its efficacy — clinically relevant for anyone actively on SJW who needs emergency contraception.

### What we don't know

Post-stopping-SJW washout time is not precisely quantified. CYP3A4 induction typically persists for 1–2 weeks after discontinuation. Direct RCTs on progestin-only pills, hormonal IUDs, or emergency contraception + SJW are lacking.

## Recommendation

If you rely on an oral contraceptive for pregnancy prevention, do not take St John's wort without discussing it with your prescriber. If you are taking both, use a reliable backup method (condom, non-hormonal IUD, abstinence) during SJW use and for at least two weeks after stopping. Breakthrough bleeding on an OC regimen that was previously stable is a warning sign — retest the situation with your prescriber before assuming it's nothing. For anyone depending on levonorgestrel emergency contraception, SJW use in the prior 1–2 weeks meaningfully reduces efficacy; consider a copper IUD as an alternative.

## Timing advice

Spacing doses does not mitigate this interaction. CYP3A4 induction is a transcriptional effect that takes days to develop and days to resolve, unrelated to the hour-by-hour timing of either pill.

## Severity: danger

Justification: DANGER applies when co-administration carries non-reversible harm. Unintended pregnancy is definitionally non-reversible. The interaction is RCT-confirmed (S1), mechanistically explained (S3), and pharmacovigilance-validated (S2, S4). This is among the best-documented herb-drug interactions in supplement literature. No downgrade is defensible.
