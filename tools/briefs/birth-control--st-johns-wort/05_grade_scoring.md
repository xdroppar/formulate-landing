# Stage 5 — GRADE-Style Evidence Scoring

Pair: **Birth control** + **St John's wort**

## Starting confidence tier

This pair has the strongest evidence base of any DANGER-tier supplement-drug interaction reviewed so far:
- 1 RCT (Pfrunder 2003, N=17, p<0.001 breakthrough bleeding)
- 1 systematic review with pharmacovigilance (Borrelli 2007)
- 1 mechanistic + clinical review (Nicolussi 2020)
- 1 recent systematic review (2024)

Starting tier for the breakthrough-bleeding claim (C1): **HIGH** (RCT-supported)
Starting tier for the pregnancy-risk claim (C2): **LOW** (case reports + pharmacovigilance)
Starting tier for the mechanism claim (C3): **HIGH** (well-characterized PXR/CYP3A4 biology)

## Claim C1 — Breakthrough bleeding

Starting: **HIGH**

| Factor | Answer | Downgrade? |
|---|---|---|
| Risk of bias | Crossover RCT, subjects as own controls. Blinding not specified but effect is large. | No |
| Inconsistency | Clear dose-response within one study. No contradicting RCT. | No |
| Indirectness | Direct — healthy women on combined OC. | No |
| Imprecision | N=17 is small BUT effect is huge (p<0.001) and dose-response is clean. Tight confidence around the direction. | Borderline; no downgrade |
| Publication bias | Single RCT — so some concern, but mechanism corroborates. | No |

**Final confidence for C1: HIGH**

## Claim C2 — Unintended pregnancy risk

Starting: **LOW** (observational)

| Factor | Answer | Upgrade? |
|---|---|---|
| Large effect | 9 pregnancies in women who had stable OC for 7+ months is a strong attribution signal. | +1 for large effect |
| Dose-response | Not measurable in case reports. | No |
| Confounders bias against effect | Pharmacovigilance selects for noticed pregnancies — confounders (missed pill, diarrhea, other interactions) would dilute, not amplify. | +1 |

**Final confidence for C2: MODERATE**

## Claim C3 — CYP3A4/P-gp induction mechanism

Starting: **HIGH**

| Factor | Answer | Downgrade? |
|---|---|---|
| Risk of bias | Well-replicated across multiple labs. | No |
| Indirectness | Direct — hyperforin as PXR agonist, validated in human hepatocytes. | No |
| Consistency | Universally accepted. | No |

**Final confidence for C3: HIGH**

## Claim C4 — Backup contraception recommendation

Derived from C1+C2+C3. Recommendation strength: MODERATE. Persistence of induction (1-2 weeks after stopping SJW) is standard CYP3A4 pharmacology.
